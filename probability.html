Probability

Axioms of probability

A probability measure is a function that assigns a real number `P(E)` to each event `E`, for which the following conditions (called "axioms") hold:

  1. For any event `E, P[E] >= 0`
  2. The measure of the entire space is `P[S] = 1`
  3. For any two mutually exclusive events `E_1` and `E_2`, `E_1 E_2 = O/`, `P[E_1 uu E_2] = P[E_1] + P[E_2]`

Definitition of statistical independence

Two events, `A` and `B`, defined on a sample space of a random experiment are independent if and only if $$ P[A \cap B] = P[A]P[B] $$ Events `A` and `B` are independent if and only if the probability of their intersection is the product `P[A]P[B]`. This means that conditional probabilities may be written as $$ P[A \, | \, B] = P[A] $$ $$ P[B \, | \, A] = P[B] $$

A random variable is a function that assigns a numerical value to each outcome in the sample space of a random experiment.

`P[X = x]` is the probability random variable `X` takes on the value x. `Pr[X = x], Prob[X = x], bbbP[X = x]` are alternative notations for `P`.
There are also different notational conventions for events e.g. `P(X = x)`, `P[X = x]`, `P{X = x}`, `P({X = x})` are all equivalent.

`E[X]` is the expected value of `X` or expectation of `X`.
`var(X)` is the variance of `X` (expected value of the square of deviations) $$ E[(X - E[X])^2]=E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 \tag{1} $$ `cov(X,Y)` is the covariance of `X` and `Y` $$ E[XY] - E[X]E[Y] \tag{2} $$ The PMF or probability mass function `p_X(x)` is the probability that the experimental value of the random variable `X` is equal to `x` on a single outcome of the experiment. $$ 0 \leq p_X(x) \leq 1 \quad \text{for all $x$} $$ $$ \sum_{x} p_X(x) = 1 $$ The PDF or probability density function `f_X(x)` is defined by $$ \mathbb{P}[a < X \leq b] = \int_{a}^{b} f_X(x) \, dx $$ The CDF or cumulative distribution function `F_X(x)` is defined by $$ F_X(x) = \mathbb{P}[X \leq x] = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f_X(y) \, dy $$
